## Phase 1 — equilibration sanity

Two independent equilibration runs + a non-local-vs-local-Kawasaki comparison,
at the working `T_i` and at a near-`T_c` point.

- **T_i = 10:** PASS. Two independent runs indistinguishable (overlap 0.900,
  KS p 0.894); non-local prep matches the local baseline (overlap 0.820). High-T
  equilibration is trivial, as expected.
- **T = 2.4 (near `T_c`):** **FAIL at 3000 sweeps** — two runs differ
  (KS p 0.003) and, tellingly, non-local-vs-local overlap is only **0.100**. The
  non-local sampler reaches the low-energy equilibrium while local Kawasaki, at
  the working budget, is still under-equilibrated (critical slowing down). This
  is the diagnostic doing its job: it confirms M2's working point (T_i=10) is
  fine **and** warns that **Milestone 4 will need substantially more preparation
  sweeps (or validated non-local prep with its own convergence check) for the
  T_i = 2.4 leg.** Recorded now so it is not discovered late.

3 seeds before the full ensemble. Final `L_S` per seed = [14.09, 13.45, 12.78],

## Phase 3 — ensemble benchmark (8 realisations)

- `E(t)` decays monotonically toward the working `E_∞(T_f) ≈ −1.883 ± 0.008`
  per spin (horizon-limited estimate from a long non-local-assisted
  equilibration at `T_f`; its N/horizon dependence is reported, never used as a
  sole diagnostic — Mpemba-claim gate).
- `S(k,t)`: the peak grows and migrates to smaller `k` as domains coarsen
  (clean, textbook).
- **Length growth — the central finding.** The *raw* effective exponent of
  `L_S` in [100, 3000] sweeps is only ≈ 0.15, well below 1/3. This is **not** a
  different growth law: it is the additive offset. Fitting the pre-registered
  `L = R₀ + (λ t)^{1/3}` (exponent fixed at 1/3):

  | estimator | R₀ | λ | R² | offset-corrected exponent |
  |---|---|---|---|---|
  | `L_C` | 0.732 ± 0.018 | 1.5e-3 | 0.9963 | 0.332 |
  | `L_S` | 4.640 ± 0.047 | 0.118 | 0.9987 | 0.331 |

  Both estimators fit the 1/3 law to R² > 0.996, and the offset-corrected
  effective exponent recovers 1/3 to better than 1%. `L_C` and `L_S` agree in
  ordering and late-time scaling (two of three; `L_E` added in M3).

## Bug found and fixed (pre-results-commit)

`analysis.fit_offset_growth` previously used a nonlinear `curve_fit` of
`(R₀, λ)`, which over a short offset-dominated window is ill-conditioned —
it returned negative `λ` with "covariance could not be estimated". Since the
pre-registration fixes the exponent at 1/3, the model is linear in `u = t^{1/3}`
(`L = R₀ + b·u`, `λ = b³`); rewrote it as a linear least-squares fit. Robust,
non-degenerate, proper errors + R². Affected results: none committed before the
fix; the bad fit never produced a recorded number.

## Surprises

1. The raw effective exponent looking "too low" is entirely an offset artefact —
   a concrete instance of exactly the failure mode the task card's offset-control
   gate is designed to catch. Worth remembering for M5: a *crossing* that
   disappears under R₀-correction is a route/offset artefact, not an inversion.
2. `L_S` carries a large offset (R₀ ≈ 4.6) because the structure-factor first
   moment assigns a finite length even to the disordered initial state (Porod
   tail). `L_C`'s offset is small (≈ 0.73). Different offsets, same exponent.
