## Intent

Assess `L(t) ~ t^{1/3}` across sizes per the reviewer checklist: finite-size
ordering, three-estimator (`L_C`, `L_S`, `L_E`) agreement, lower-cutoff
sensitivity, a window-below-saturation guard, and per-N manifests + a summary.

## Method (the honest exponent question)

The pre-registration fixes the growth exponent at 1/3, so the **confirmatory**
test is: *does* `L = R₀ + (λt)^{1/3}` *fit?* — measured by the R² of the linear
fit of `L` vs `t^{1/3}`. Separately, as a **descriptive** robustness check, a
free exponent is estimated by scanning α and maximising the linearity (R²) of
`L` vs `t^α`; its reported band is the range of α within an R² tolerance of the
maximum. At N ≤ 128 this band is deliberately wide — the task card's "thin for a
precision exponent" regime — so the free exponent is reported as illustrative,
never as a precision measurement.

## Results

**Consistency with the 1/3 law — strong, all N, all estimators.** The
`R₀+(λt)^{1/3}` fit gives R² ≥ 0.992 everywhere (mostly > 0.997): `L_S` is the
best behaved (R² = 0.999, 0.999, 0.9995 at N = 32, 64, 128). Two-of-three
estimator agreement holds at every size.

**Free exponent — near 1/3 but illustrative.** Point estimates scatter in
0.34–0.45 (bands ≈ ±0.04), tending slightly *above* 1/3 (not below, which would
signal finite-size slowdown). `L_S` is the most consistent (0.355, 0.380, 0.365).
`L_C` is noisier at small N because at early times `L_C ≲ 1` lattice spacing
(discretisation-limited). The slight high bias is the expected artefact of
fitting a single power law over a finite, offset-affected, *early*-time window,
where the effective exponent is still rising through 1/3 toward the asymptote.

**Lower-cutoff sensitivity.** The fixed-1/3 fit R² stays > 0.99 across all
cutoffs {50…800} at every N (cutoff-stable in the confirmatory sense). The free
α drifts with cutoff at N=32 (0.335→0.415) but is stable at N=128 (0.36→0.375) —
larger N ⇒ longer window ⇒ better-constrained exponent, as expected.

**Window below saturation.** max `L_S/N` = 0.357 (N=32), 0.239 (N=64), 0.144
(N=128), all under the 0.40 guard. Smaller N sits closer to saturation — the
finite-size effect made explicit. No window was trimmed.

**E_∞ finite-size dependence (reported, per the gate).** Working `E_∞(N,T_f)`
per spin: −1.788 (N=32), −1.882 (N=64), −1.918 (N=128); more negative with N
(less relative interface), each from a 10 000-sweep non-local-assisted
equilibration ± its sample SD. Used only to derive `L_E`, never as a sole
diagnostic.

**Finite-size scaling collapse.** The *naive* `L_S/N` vs `t/N³` curves do **not**
collapse — they separate by ≈ `R₀/N` (= 0.14, 0.072, 0.036), i.e. the offset is
not scaled out. After subtracting the per-N `R₀`, `(L_S−R₀)/N` vs `t/N³`
**collapses onto a common master curve** in the scaling region `t/N³ ≳ 10⁻⁴`.
This both confirms the `L ~ N·f(t/N³)` form expected for 1/3 growth and is a
clean demonstration of why offset control matters (directly relevant to the M5
crossing test: an apparent inversion that vanishes under `R₀`-correction is an
offset/route artefact).

## Verdict

Milestone-3 **demonstrator** and **robustness** criteria met: a pre-saturation
window consistent with `L ~ t^{1/3}` (R² > 0.997 for `L_S`), local effective
exponents and cutoff sensitivity reported, three-estimator agreement, finite-size
comparison across 32/64/128 with the window below the equilibrium domain scale,
and an offset-corrected FSS collapse. The exponent is stated as illustrative
(≈ 1/3, not precisely pinned at these sizes), per the task card.

## Surprises / notes

1. The naive FSS collapse failing is not a defect — it is the offset signature,
   and the offset-corrected collapse recovering a master curve is the
   confirmation. Logged as the M3 analogue of the M2 offset lesson.
2. `L_C` is the least reliable estimator at these sizes (sub-spacing early
   values); `L_S` is the most reliable; `L_E` depends on `E_∞` and tracks it.
   The two-of-three gate exists precisely because no single estimator is clean.
