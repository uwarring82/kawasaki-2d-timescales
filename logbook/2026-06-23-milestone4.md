**Author:** M4 session
**Software version:** v0.1.0
**Runs:** `results/m4_equilibration_v1/` (Phase A gate),
`results/m4_sweep_v1/` (Phase B sweep) — each with its own manifest.
**Config:** `configs/temperature_sweep_m4.yaml` — N=64, M=0, T_f=1.36 (0.6 T_c),
T_i ∈ {2.4, 2.8, 3.5, 5.0, 10.0}, 12 realisations, common schedule (8000 sweeps).

## Intent

Produce ensemble post-quench trajectories for the T_i grid at a common T_f, on
initial states *validated* to be equilibrated — starting (per the reviewer)
with the near-T_c T_i=2.4 leg flagged in M2.

## Conserved-dynamics clarification

Kawasaki dynamics conserves M exactly: M ≡ 0 here by construction (asserted each
chain). There is therefore **no magnetisation distribution to equilibrate** (it
is a delta at 0). The equilibration observables are the **energy** and the
**correlation length L_C** (the long-wavelength check), plus the energy
autocorrelation time.

## Phase A — equilibration gate (and resolving the M2 flag)

First, the M2 "T_i=2.4 under-equilibrated" alarm was diagnosed and **resolved**:
it was (a) a correlated-sampling artifact — M2's `compare_equilibration` sampled
the energy every 10 sweeps, *below* the autocorrelation time τ_E≈24–37, so the
KS test was invalid — and (b) a non-local-vs-*local* comparison in which *local*
Kawasaki (not our prep kernel) was the laggard. An independent-chains test
(decorrelated, one sample per chain) shows the non-local prep equilibrates the
energy by ~1500 sweeps at T=2.4.

**Gate method.** For each T_i: K=40 independent chains run from random starts for
a candidate budget B; their fully-independent final samples of energy and L_C
are compared (means) against a long, decorrelated reference equilibrium
distribution. A budget *converges* when both means lie within 4 combined SEM of
the reference (mean stability is robust; KS p-values are reported as supporting
evidence but are noisy at modest K near T_c). Gated budget = smallest converged
candidate, raised to cover ≥ 30·τ_E.

**A gate-logic bug, found and fixed.** The first criterion ("smallest budget
such that *all larger* candidates also pass KS") was brittle: with K=30, KS
p-values fluctuate around α near T_c (large near-critical fluctuations), so a
single chance failure at B=8000 spuriously failed the whole T_i=2.4 leg. Replaced
with the mean-stability criterion above; this is QC methodology (not the M5
hypothesis test), and the change is recorded here.

**Result — all five legs green** (mean gaps ≤ 1.4σ for both E and L_C, vs the
4σ gate). τ_E shows clean critical slowing down toward T_c but stays modest under
the non-local kernel:

| T_i | τ_E (sweeps) | gated prep budget |
|---|---|---|
| 10.0 | 2.6 | 250 |
| 5.0 | 2.0 | 250 |
| 3.5 | 2.9 | 500 |
| 2.8 | 6.8 | 1000 |
| 2.4 | 36.9 | 2000 |

T_i=10 hot reference matches the high-T expansion ⟨e⟩ ≈ −2/T = −0.20 (measured
−0.204): it genuinely randomises to the expected canonical baseline. Budgets are
recorded in `results/m4_equilibration_v1/budgets.json` and echoed in the sweep
manifest's seed block.

## Phase B — common-protocol sweep

Common protocol: identical T_f, N, schedule, and **local-Kawasaki kinetic
kernel**; only T_i (and its gated prep budget) varies. Common
E_∞(T_f) = −1.882 ± 0.009 per spin.

| T_i | initial L_C (post-prep) | final L_S (t=8000) |
|---|---|---|
| 2.4 | 2.43 | 27.19 |
| 2.8 | 1.10 | 18.53 |
| 3.5 | 0.85 | 16.28 |
| 5.0 | 0.74 | 15.18 |
| 10.0 | 0.67 | 14.77 |

- **Initial-ξ ordering confirmed.** The coldest above-T_c prep (2.4) starts with
  the largest correlation length (2.43); the hottest (10) the smallest (0.67),
  with a sharp rise near T_c — the "starting ahead" structure that makes an
  inversion non-trivial.
- **No raw inversion in this window.** The `L_S(t)` curves are strictly ordered
  by T_i and their 95% CI bands never cross: the colder preparation leads at all
  times. The directional hypothesis (hotter overtakes) is **not** supported in
  the *raw* length within 8000 sweeps at N=64.

## M5-relevant flags

1. The colder lead may be largely the *offset* R₀(T_i) (colder starts with a
   larger domain scale). The real Mpemba test is whether the hot preparation's
   *offset-corrected* (L−R₀) overtakes — to be done in M5 with the
   difference-bootstrap, not read off the raw curves here.
2. T_i=2.4 reaches L_S ≈ 27 ≈ 0.42·N at N=64 — at/above the saturation guard, so
   at N=64 the cold leg saturates within the schedule. The M5 crossing window
   must stay below saturation; the pre-registered primary pair is at N=128
   (saturation further out), which will need its own budget calibration.
