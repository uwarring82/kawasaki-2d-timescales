## VERDICT — no supported inversion

A clean, well-controlled **negative** result: the directional hypothesis (the
hotter preparation overtakes the colder one) is **not supported**, and the
colder lead is **not** an offset artefact masking a hidden hot-overtake.

## What was run (guardrails)

1. **N=128 gate, τ_E measured directly** (not scaled): T_i=10 τ_E=2.1→budget 250;
   T_i=2.4 τ_E=39.6 (cf. 36.9 at N=64)→budget 2000. Both legs converged
   (mean gaps ≤ 2.2σ). Prep budgets recorded in the gate + sweep manifests.
2. **Common protocol**: identical T_f, N, schedule, and local-Kawasaki kinetic
   kernel; only T_i (and its gated budget) varies.
3. **Saturation guard satisfied for BOTH legs**: max L_S/N = 0.128 (hot), 0.244
   (cold), below the 0.40 guard. N=128 bought the headroom the N=64 cold leg
   lacked (0.42 there).
4. **Verdict on the offset-corrected difference** (amendment A1):
   `D(t) = (L_hot − R0_hot) − (L_cold − R0_cold)`, R0 re-fitted inside each of
   5000 bootstrap resamples.

## Evidence

Raw `L_S(t)`: the cold leg leads the hot leg throughout (final 31.3 vs 16.4),
the CI bands never cross — **no raw overtaking** (raw `L_hot − L_cold` is
negative with no sign change for all three estimators; the pre-registered
crossing rule yields no crossing).

Offset-corrected difference `D(t)` (the verdict quantity), at t = 15000:

| estimator | R0_hot | R0_cold | D_final | 95% CI | FDR-significant | sign |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `L_S` | 4.67 | 19.53 | −0.019 | [−0.353, +0.309] | no | **null** |
| `L_C` | 0.67 | 2.14 | −0.742 | [−0.855, −0.639] | yes | **−** |
| `L_E` | 0.72 | 1.42 | −1.453 | [−1.580, −1.332] | yes | **−** |

- **No estimator's `D` is positive** — there is zero support for the hot
  preparation overtaking after the head-start is removed.
- `L_S` (the cleanest domain-size measure) gives `D ≈ 0`: the dominant length
  coarsens at the **same rate** for both preparations after offset removal —
  physically expected, since the growth amplitude λ is set by the common T_f
  kinetics, not by T_i.
- `L_C` and `L_E` give `D < 0`, robustly (BH-FDR across time, CIs exclude zero):
  the colder near-critical preparation stays **ahead** even after offset removal
  in the morphology-sensitive estimators. These estimators retain a memory of
  the more ramified near-critical initial structure that the dominant-length
  `L_S` does not — a concrete instance of the estimator-specific systematics the
  two-of-three rule exists to handle.

**Two-of-three**: `L_C` and `L_E` agree (offset-corrected `D < 0`); `L_S` null.
At least two estimators agree, and none favours the hot leg.

**Resolving power (not variance-limited).** Minimum detectable offset-corrected
difference at power 0.8: `L_C` 0.15, `L_E` 0.18, `L_S` 0.47 — all small relative
to the length scales (10–30). The `L_C`/`L_E` differences are clearly resolved
(CIs exclude zero); the `L_S` null is a genuine null, not an underpowered one.
So the outcome is **no_supported_inversion**, not **underdetermined**.

## Four-outcome scheme

- **Spectral Mpemba** — not awardable at N=128; the small-N (4×4)
  exact-diagonalisation spectral probe is a separate deliverable, **not run this
  session** (noted, not silently omitted).
- **Coarsening-route inversion** — **not** awarded: no raw crossing, and the
  offset-corrected difference favours hot in *no* estimator.
- **No supported inversion** — **AWARDED.** No crossing survives uncertainty,
  estimator, size (N=128 below saturation), offset (`R0`), and time-origin

  leg (`L_C`, `L_E`).
- **Underdetermined** — not applicable; differences are resolved.

## Interpretation

At this operational point (`M=0`, `T_f = 0.6 T_c`, `N = 128`), conserved
Kawasaki coarsening shows **no Mpemba-like inversion** for the primary pair. The
colder, near-critical preparation starts ahead (larger ξ) and stays ahead; the
offset-control analysis shows this is a genuine lead, not an artefact, and there
is no hidden rate advantage for the hot preparation (`L_S` rate equal; `L_C`,
`L_E` morphology favouring cold). This is consistent with the task card's framing
that, in the scaling regime, both preparations share the same late-time scaling
form up to non-universal amplitudes/offsets — a statistically defensible
deflationary result, which the card explicitly admits as valid and complete.
