## Overall assessment

The scoped headline stands: for the designated primary pair (`T_i=10` vs `2.4`,
`T_f=0.6 T_c`, `N=128`, `M=0`), I found no blocker that flips the conclusion
from `no_supported_inversion` to a hot-overtake Mpemba effect. The engine and
spectral tier are reproducible, the primary fixed-1/3 offset analysis is stable
to neighboring fixed exponents, and the spectral no-Mpemba result reproduces.

The main caveat is important but not verdict-flipping: an unconstrained
free-exponent offset scan makes `L_S` positive and FDR-significant, although
`L_C` and `L_E` remain significantly negative. The release should therefore
avoid wording that the hot leg is favored in "no estimator" under all reasonable
offset models; that phrase is true for the pre-registered fixed-1/3 analysis,
not for the free-exponent stress test. The no-inversion headline remains valid
because the two-of-three hot-inversion rule is still not met.

## Issues

### Major - free-exponent offset correction weakens the strongest C4 wording

The pre-registered fixed-exponent analysis is internally consistent, but the
review brief specifically asks whether the offset correction survives a
free-exponent re-fit. My stress test re-fitted `R0` inside each bootstrap using
a linearity scan over `alpha in [0.20, 0.50]` and `n_boot=5000` for the decisive
`L_S` case. Result:

| estimator | free-alpha `D_final` | 95% CI | FDR late sign |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| `L_C` | `-1.408` | `[-1.529, -1.295]` | negative |
| `L_S` | `+0.401` | `[+0.115, +0.676]` | positive |
| `L_E` | `-1.568` | `[-1.683, -1.460]` | negative |

For `L_S`, the selected free exponents were `alpha_hot=0.345` and
`alpha_cold=0.375`, with `R^2` values above `0.99996`. This does not satisfy a
two-of-three hot-inversion criterion, but it does refute the stronger robustness
claim that no estimator can favor the hot leg once the exponent is allowed to
float. The publication wording should say "no estimator under the pre-registered
fixed-1/3 offset model" or report this free-exponent `L_S` behavior explicitly.

### Minor - C2 free-exponent language is too strong if read literally

The fixed `L = R0 + (lambda t)^(1/3)` fits are excellent (`R^2 >= 0.992` across
`N=32,64,128` and all three estimators). However, several preferred-alpha bands
from the free-exponent linearity scan do not include `1/3` even with the script's
`0.02` margin: for example `N=64 L_C` reports `0.45 [0.41,0.49]`, `N=64 L_E`
reports `0.425 [0.385,0.46]`, and `N=128 L_C` reports
`0.405 [0.37,0.435]`. The offset-corrected effective exponents are closer to
`1/3`, and `L_S` is better behaved, so this is a wording caveat, not a coarsening
law failure. The defensible claim is that the fixed 1/3 law fits well and the
free exponent is illustrative and finite-window biased, not that every free
exponent estimate is statistically consistent with `1/3`.

### Minor - "spectral predicts coarsening" should remain qualitative

The exact `4x4` spectral tier is solid, but it is not the same observable as the
large-`N` coarsening crossing. I would keep "predicts" only if immediately
qualified as a qualitative consistency statement. The evidence supports "both
tiers point away from a hot-overtake"; it does not derive the `N=128` coarsening
verdict from the `4x4` spectral overlap.

## Claim verdicts

### C1 - Engine correctness: Confirmed

I re-ran the full suite at the release tag in both default and no-Numba modes;
both returned `70 passed`. Code inspection agrees with the tests:
`delta_E_for_swap` implements `Delta E = (s_i - s_j)(a - b)`, local Kawasaki
uses `N^2` attempted updates per sweep, equal-spin swaps count as no-op attempts,
and the random stream is generated outside the JIT path. The exact `4x4`
canonical histogram tests cover both local Kawasaki and the non-local
preparation sampler.

### C2 - Coarsening law: Confirmed with caveats

The saved M3 artifacts support the fixed 1/3 law strongly: all reported
fixed-exponent fit `R^2` values exceed `0.99`, and the offset-corrected
`(L_S-R0)/N` versus `t/N^3` collapse is documented. Caveat: the free-alpha scan
is not uniformly centered on `1/3`; it should remain illustrative and should not
be used as an independent precision exponent claim.

### C3 - Equilibration gate: Confirmed with caveats

The M4 and M5 gates use independent-chain mean stability for energy and `L_C`.
At the primary `N=128` point, the gate reports `T_i=10` budget `250` and
`T_i=2.4` budget `2000`, with both candidates passing energy and `L_C` mean
criteria; `T_i=2.4` has measured `tau_E=39.64` sweeps and a `30*tau_E` floor of
`1190`, so the chosen budget is conservative by that metric. I accept the change
from the noisy KS criterion as QC, not hypothesis testing, but it should continue
to be described as a methodological amendment.

### C4 - Coarsening verdict: Confirmed with major caveat

The pre-registered fixed-1/3 offset result reproduces the release report:
`L_C D_final=-0.742` with CI `[-0.855,-0.639]`, `L_S D_final=-0.019` with CI
`[-0.353,+0.309]`, and `L_E D_final=-1.453` with CI `[-1.580,-1.332]`.
Bootstrap stability is adequate: rerunning the original fixed-1/3 bootstrap at
`n_boot=1000` and `5000` changed final CIs only modestly and did not change
significance.

Neighboring fixed exponents do not change the verdict:

| alpha | `L_C` | `L_S` | `L_E` |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| `0.30` | negative significant | null | negative significant |
| `1/3` | negative significant | null | negative significant |
| `0.36` | negative significant | null | negative significant |

The free-exponent caveat above prevents calling the stronger "no estimator
favors hot" statement robust across offset models. It still does not produce a
two-of-three hot inversion.

On FDR scope, the primary-pair time-point BH-FDR is adequate for the scoped
headline. It does not support any broader claim across the deferred full
`(T_i,T_f,N)` grid.

On resolving power, the release's MDD framing is reasonable for the observed
signals: `L_C` and `L_E` are resolved, while `L_S` can rule out moderate
hot-favoring effects at roughly the `0.47` length scale but not arbitrarily tiny
ones.

Caveat: `4x4` is exact but tiny, and the spectral/coarsening link is qualitative

## Section-4 weak points addressed

1. **Offset correction self-reference:** neighboring fixed exponents are safe;
   free exponent creates a positive `L_S` caveat but not a two-of-three hot
   inversion.
2. **One operational point:** confirmed. The no-inversion headline must remain
   scoped to the primary pair and cannot claim absence across the full grid.
3. **Morphology memory:** `D<0` in `L_C`/`L_E` is a measurement; "morphology
   memory" is an interpretation. It could also be called a cold-faster or
   anti-Mpemba tendency in those estimators. This does not matter for the
   directional hot-overtake headline, but the explanation should be labelled.
4. **`n=48`:** sufficient to resolve `L_C` and `L_E`; `L_S` is a credible null
   only above the reported MDD scale, not a proof of exactly equal rates.
5. **Spectral/coarsening agreement:** confirmed as qualitative consistency, not
   a derivation.
6. **`4x4` spectral size:** exact and robust within the enumerated sector, but
   far from thermodynamic; sparse-Krylov larger-sector work remains the natural
   next hardening step.
