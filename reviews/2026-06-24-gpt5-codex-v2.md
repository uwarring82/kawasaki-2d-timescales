## Physics and modelling

**Verdict: Confirmed with caveats.**

The model and kinetic clock match the stated Kawasaki-Ising scope: square
periodic lattice, fixed `M=0`, local nearest-neighbour exchange after the quench,
Metropolis acceptance, and one sweep as `N^2` attempted updates independent of
acceptance. The `dE` formula is derived in `docs/physics.md` and validated
against brute-force energy recomputation. The non-local opposite-spin
preparation sampler is kept separate from the post-quench local kinetic kernel
and is checked against the same fixed-sector equilibrium.

Caveat: the 4x4 spectral tier is a legitimate finite-state spectral diagnostic,
but it is not a derivation of the N=128 coarsening result. The technical README
now states this correctly as qualitative consistency (`README.md:153`-`162`).
The plain-language section still says the exact calculation "predicts" the
large-size result (`README.md:51`-`52`), which is stronger than the evidence
requires. Severity: **minor** wording issue.

## Numerics and statistics

**Verdict: Confirmed with caveats.**

I independently re-ran the M5 `--analyse` stage from the committed ensemble
arrays. It reproduced the central coarsening verdict:

| estimator | offset-corrected final D | 95% CI | late FDR significant | interpretation |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| `L_C` | -0.742 | [-0.855, -0.639] | yes | cold remains ahead |
| `L_S` | -0.019 | [-0.353, +0.309] | no | null |
| `L_E` | -1.453 | [-1.580, -1.332] | yes | cold remains ahead |

My direct array check also found no raw mean overtaking in any estimator. At the
final checkpoint, raw means were `L_S_hot=16.40` and `L_S_cold=31.28`; max
`L_S/N` was 0.128 hot and 0.244 cold, below the 0.40 finite-size guard.

The statistical conclusion therefore stands for the designated primary pair:
no raw crossing, and after offset removal no two-of-three hot-favouring
estimator agreement. The current offset-sensitivity artifact is important:
under a free-exponent stress test, `L_S` can flip positive, but `L_C` and `L_E`
remain negative, so the two-of-three hot-inversion rule is still not met.

Caveats:

- The full pre-registered `(T_i,T_f,N)` coarsening grid is deferred. The
  published conclusion should remain scoped to the primary `N=128`,
  `T_i=10` vs `2.4`, `T_f=0.6 T_c`, `M=0` point.
- The achieved ensemble is 48 realisations per leg, below the pre-registration's
  expected `O(1e2-1e3)`. The resolved negative `L_C`/`L_E` signs support
  `no_supported_inversion`, but the `L_S` null should not be described as a
  tightly powered zero beyond its reported CI.
- The M5 report stores `oc_late_sign` as the sign of the late-window mean even
  when no late FDR-significant point exists (`scripts/milestone5_crossing.py:188`).
  This is harmless for the verdict because `_verdict` also checks
  `oc_late_fdr_significant`, but it is easy to misread. Severity: **minor**.

fully evidenced in the repository. Severity: **minor** for the scientific
claim, **publication-blocking only if an archival DOI is required before release
promotion**.

## Overall assessment

The headline conclusion stands as scoped: for `M=0`, `T_f=0.6 T_c`, `N=128`,
and the primary `T_i=10` vs `2.4` pair, the evidence supports
**no supported Mpemba-like inversion**. The colder preparation leads in raw
domain length throughout; after offset removal, two estimators significantly
favour the colder leg and the clean `L_S` estimator is null, not hot-favouring
under the pre-registered model. The independent 4x4 spectral probe points in
the same no-inversion direction. Before considering this publishable as more
than a scoped negative computational result, I would require either completion
of the declared grid or very explicit wording that the wider grid is deferred,
plus cleanup of the DOI placeholder and the minor report-sign ambiguity.
