## Independent confirmation of the major caveat

Headline fixed-1/3 offset analysis is **stable** across neighbouring fixed exponents:

| α | L_C | L_S | L_E |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0.30 | negative significant | null | negative significant |
| 1/3 | negative significant | null | negative significant |
| 0.36 | negative significant | null | negative significant |

Under a **free-exponent** offset fit inside the bootstrap, `L_S` flips to favour
the hot leg:

| Estimator | Free-α `D_final` | 95% CI | Sign |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| `L_C` | −1.408 | [−1.529, −1.295] | − |
| `L_S` | **+0.401** | **[+0.092, +0.664]** | **+** |
| `L_E` | −1.568 | [−1.684, −1.462] | − |

This does **not** meet the two-of-three hot-inversion rule, so the scoped
`no_supported_inversion` verdict survives — but it refutes the stronger wording
"no estimator favours hot under all reasonable offset models."

## Bottom line

The existing (Codex) review is sound. **No blocker; the scoped
`no_supported_inversion` headline stands**, with the important non-verdict-
flipping caveat that the free-exponent offset model makes `L_S` favour the hot
leg. Publication wording should qualify "no estimator favours hot" to "no
estimator favours hot under the pre-registered fixed-1/3 offset model."
